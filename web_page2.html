Which City?

Mbombela is the capital city of the Mpumalanga Province. It’s a gateway to Kruger National Park, home to elephants, zebras, rhinos and other wildlife. The city’s Lowveld National Botanical Garden features a man-made rainforest. Northwest are the prehistoric Sudwala Caves, with unusual rock formations. South, Umhloti Nature Reserve contains the Jane Goodall Institute’s Chimp Eden sanctuary. I love nature and I believe it would be great to visit Mbombela and see all the streams and valleys. Get to see most of the wild animals on a free-range. I visited the city when I was young and can barely remember anything.

Historic places in my city.

Union Buildings Gardens

The Union Buildings Gardens is a national heritage site situated in the capital city of Pretoria. It is a peaceful and beautiful garden that is perfect for picnics and family outings.The ground holds various monuments, statues and memorials. It the statue of the first black president of South Africa ,Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela.

SANDF Wall of Remembrance

The South African National Defense Force (SANDF) Wall of Remembrance is a tribute to the fallen heroes of South Africa. It is a solemn and respectful place where people can pay their respects and honor those who have given their lives for their country.The sculpture commemorates the courage of the Voortrekker women and their families who survived the Great Trek and made the eventual settlement of the interior possible. It has a lot of lively history that I could learn.

Burgers Park

Burgers Park is a beautiful park located in the heart of Pretoria. It has lush green lawns, beautiful flowers, and various trees, making it the perfect place for a picnic, a walk, or just to relax and enjoy nature. It is the oldest park in the city. It holds the statue of the President Burgers. It would be a great pleasure to one day spend a day there with family.
